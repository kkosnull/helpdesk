Hijet Diagnostic Engine Warning Light Flash Codes

If your engine warning light is on, or comes on occasionally then you need to do some diagnostic work. This is the instructions for how to do the diagnostic check procedure on a Daihatsu Hijet.

Firstly bridge the connectors as shown in this diagram, the connector is in the passenger side footwell, and the wire the pins that need connecting are the Black/White and Brown wires. Do it while the key is fully off, then turn on the key, do not start the engine. The engine check light will flash in bursts, there is a 4 second gap between each code, each code has two digits and each number is separated by 2.5 second gap. So basically four flashes, short gap, three flashes, long gap, five flashes, short gap, four flashes, long gap, etc repeat….   equals codes 43 and 54. These codes are the same for the Daihatsu Hijet 993cc efi, and the 1300cc versions, and the Piaggio Porter versions.

Forum members have commented that the best way to bridge/short the connector is to put a pair of female spades on a short piece of wire, they will then slip over the connectors in the diagnostics connector and won’t fall out. Better than messing around with a bit of wire that keeps getting dislodged.

 

For the 1.3 Hijet, the diagnostic connector is in a slightly different place, it is under the dashboard on the drivers side behind the fuse box, tucked in just under the carpet with also a blue coloured connector taped together. To show you its location and how to jumper it over, I have taken some photos with the dashboard removed to give you a better idea of its location. Once you have found the connector, simply bridge the black/white and brown/white pins together using some welding wire or a paper clip. (click on the photos to see a bigger picture)

 

OLD INFORMATION BELOW!! – Thanks to xxemaphorr for finally finding the diagnostic connector!!

Originally on the 1300cc efi Hijet/Porter I could not find the diagnostic connector anywhere, So I came up with the following work around which is now redundant thanks to the new information above by xxemaphorr. The solution I had found to get the ECU into diagnostic mode was quite simple. Firstly I took the ECU apart and worked out which pin was labelled as T, it is the brown wire as pictured, then I pushed a bit of wire up the connector and connected the other end of the bit of wire to the ECU case to ground T to activate diagnostic mode. This works perfectly, when you turn on the ignition you get a flashing check engine light, and when I disconnected the MAP sensor as a test, it gave me code 31.

 

 


List of error codes and definitions:-

  • 13 – Rev signal – Crank position sensor fault
  • 14 – Camshaft position sensor Fault
  • 16 – Ignition signal – Igniter unit  or wiring
  • 17 – Random/multiple Misfire Detected
  • 18 – Knock sensor fault
  • 21 – Primary Oxygen sensor signal – open or shorted (lambda sensor in exhaust)
  • 22 – Secondary Oxygen sensor  - open or shorted
  • 23 – Primary Oxygen sensor heater fault
  • 24 – Secondary Oxygen sensor heater fault
  • 25 – Running too lean
  • 26 – Running too rich
  • 27 – Catalytic convertor efficiency below threshold
  • 31 – Pressure sensor – open or shorted MAP sensor circuit
  • 32 – Atmospheric pressure sensor  (BARO) malfunction
  • 36 - Random/multiple Misfire Detected
  • 41 – Throttle position sensor signal – open or shorted (on throttle body assembly)
  • 42 – Coolant temperature sensor – open or shorted
  • 43 – Intake air temperature sensor signal – open or shorted
  • 51 – Switch Signal – Caused by throttle position sensor
  • 52 – Vehicle speed sensor signal – open or shorted
  • 54 – Starter Signal – open or shorted or due to bump start
  • 61 – Engine Control Module – Power Supply
  • 71 – Idle Speed Control System – Fault
  • 73 – Camshaft Position Actuator – Circuit Fault
  • 74 – Oil control Valve – Circuit Fault
  • 75 - Camshaft Position Actuator – Circuit Fault
  • 76 – Evaporative Emission canister purge valve – Circuit Fault
